01 · The Thesis

Stop selling to shops. Sell to the people who own the shops.

One-at-a-time SME sales crush technical founders. NuAi's architecture — in particular, the Group Portal that ships with it — is purpose-built for the three sales motions that actually scale in South Africa: franchise channel, accounting-firm channel, and industry-body partnerships. The code to run a 200-outlet network is already on disk. The work that remains is commercial.

What NuAi is. A multi-tenant SaaS ERP that adapts to four industries from one codebase: cannabis retail, cannabis dispensary, food & beverage, and general retail. Full IFRS financials, POS, inventory with AVCO costing, HR, loyalty, and QR authentication — plus a live AI layer (Smart Capture, ingredient ingest, ProteaAI) and a franchise Group Portal with cross-store analytics.

Why it matters commercially. No South African product combines SA-native regulatory depth (R638 allergens, SAHPRA Schedule 6, SARS VAT 201 auto-pipeline, BCEA leave) with AI ingest and a multi-store franchise architecture in one platform. The closest global comparables (Apicbase, GrowerIQ, SAP Business One) each do one piece and cost three to twenty times as much.

The plan. Three parallel channels, ordered by expected payback: a franchise pilot landed through FASA / Absa Franchise Banking, an accounting-firm reseller relationship, and a public reference campaign built around a formalised small-retailer pilot. Unit-economics modelled before first paid contract. Pricing validated against COGS, not intuition.

NuAi isn’t competing with POS tools. It’s competing with the spreadsheet the franchisor runs royalties on, the email thread the accountant uses to chase client documents, and the four different apps a restaurant owner logs into every morning to run their business. — The competitive frame that actually sells

03 · The South African Market

A market that is both large enough to fund a category leader and underserved by the right shape of product.

South Africa’s business-software market is simultaneously crowded at the edges and hollow in the middle. The edges have either commodity accounting (Sage Pastel, Xero) at R200–800 per seat per month, or enterprise ERP (SAP Business One) at R10,000+ per seat per month. The middle — the R3,500 to R15,000 per month, multi-function, compliance-aware, AI-native band that SMEs and franchise groups actually need — is effectively empty. That is the price window NuAi was designed for.

Four verticals, four stories

Food & Beverage

South Africa has roughly 85,000 restaurants and 850 food-service franchise systems. None have an integrated AI ingredient-ingest + R638 compliance + HACCP + POS + financials stack from one vendor. Apicbase (the global gold standard) is Belgian, not SA-present, and starts at around £1,500–£2,500 per site per month. NuAi ships that capability at roughly a third of the price, fluent in SA regulation from day one.

Cannabis Dispensary

SAHPRA has issued 120 medical cannabis licences with 93 active. The commercial cannabis framework (Hemp and Cannabis Commercialisation Policy) lands at Cabinet in April 2026 with a Parliamentary Bill targeted for mid-2027. Operators who adopt robust cannabis compliance software before the licensing window opens start with two years of clean records on day one of commercial approval. NuAi already ships Schedule 6 void-only dispensing logs, S21 patient tracking, HPCSA doctor fields, and a Controlled Substance Register. This is the strongest moat in the product.

Professional Services & Trade (expansion)

Neither vertical ships today — both are natural extensions of the existing architecture. The industry-profile system already handles four verticals; adding a fifth (professional_services) and a sixth (trade_services) is an estimated 60–90 hours each, not a platform rewrite. See §06 for the expansion roadmap.

Franchise Groups (the channel)

727 franchise systems. 68,463 franchisees. 15% of GDP. Multi-unit ownership rising, brands favouring experienced operators. The Group Portal is already built for this. One franchisor adoption = 5 to 200 tenants on the platform for the sales effort of a single SME deal. This is where the economics of one-at-a-time SME acquisition break and channel sales win.

Regulatory Timing

The cannabis commercial licensing window opens in 2027–2028. Every month between now and then is a month NuAi’s early dispensary customers accumulate audit-ready records that their competitors will not have. Time-to-compliance is a defensible moat that expires — and it expires in our favour.

05 · Defensibility

Five moats, ranked honestly by how hard each is to replicate.

Anyone can write a React frontend and a Postgres schema. The five things below are not in that category. They represent either long regulatory-research investments, infrastructure advantages with real switching costs, or timing windows that close. Strength bars are self-assessed against the question “how long would a well-funded competitor need to match this?”

01

SA-native regulatory compliance depth

R638 food allergens with intrinsic-containment flags, SAHPRA Schedule 6 void-only dispensing, SARS VAT 201 auto-population, BCEA leave patterns, HPCSA prescriber fields, 40 SA public holidays seeded. Replication cost is not the code — it’s the 12+ months of SA-specific research to know which fields matter, which regulations govern, and which formats are inspector-acceptable.

High · ~12mo
02

Cannabis regulatory timing window

Commercial licensing opens 2027–2028. Every tenant NuAi onboards today accumulates Schedule 6 void-only logs, perpetual CSR ledger, S21 authorisation history. Competitors who enter the category after licensing start their customers on day zero of records. This moat closes — in our favour — as the window approaches.

High · expiring
03

Industry-profile architecture

One codebase, four profiles, live. Most vertical SaaS either forks per vertical or glues modules onto a generic core. NuAi’s runtime-branching profile system is a genuinely difficult architectural achievement: 26 files with profile branching, getWaterfall() producing four distinct nav topologies, revenue sources that differ per profile (dispensary from dispensing_log, F&B from orders). Replicable but non-trivial — 6–9 months minimum.

Medium · ~6mo
04

AI that is in the workflow, not on top of it

Smart Capture, ingredient ingest, ProteaAI, the nightly loyalty engine — Claude is a code-path citizen, not a chat widget bolted onto a legacy app. Switching cost for a tenant who has run six months of Smart-Capture bookkeeping is meaningful: their expense journal history, VAT trail, and auto-generated entries all live here. Replicable technically; switching-cost moat grows with tenure.

Medium · compounding
05

Group-Portal channel readiness

tenant_groups and tenant_group_members are live schema with a working NetworkDashboard, per-store comparison grid, combined stock value at AVCO, and inter-store transfer hooks. The royalty_percentage field exists in schema; UI to surface it arrives in the next franchise-feature sprint. Most ERPs either don’t have a group view or sell it as a six-figure enterprise add-on. NuAi ships it at standard pricing. This is the asset that makes channel sales economically viable.

Medium · widening

07 · Go-to-Market Playbooks

Three channels. One is the main road, two are the offramps.

These are not “options to consider.” They are sequences with concrete 30 / 60 / 90 milestones that a solo founder can actually run in parallel. Each playbook specifies the access mechanism, the pitch angle, the expected deal size, the concrete first moves, and the known failure modes.

Main Road

Playbook A · The Franchise Channel

Expected ARR per dealR200k – R2.4M

Target profile

SA franchise systems, 5 to 50 outlets, multi-industry. F&B preferred first (we already have the demo assets). Start mid-size — enterprise (100+) wants RFP processes we do not yet have staffed.

Pitch angle

“Your stores run a patchwork of five tools. NuAi is one login. Free 90-day pilot across HQ + 2 stores. If it doesn’t save you one EHP inspection and 20 hours per month, we refund everything.” The Group Portal demo is the closing moment.

How to access them

FASA Professional Service Provider membership (annual fee is small; directory presence is large). Absa Franchise Banking warm intros — they publish sector reports and know every franchisor. Whichfranchise.co.za directory as a cold-outreach list. Eric Parker (FranchisingPlus) and Vera Valasis (Absa) as known gatekeepers.

Known failure modes

Franchisors are conservative. Sales cycles run 6 to 18 months. Expect three meetings per deal minimum. A pilot that cannot survive a first-meeting demo of the full SA-native compliance surface will fail — polish 2B.3/2B.4 (ingredient ingest UI) and the R638 allergen demo before the first franchise meeting.

Offramp #2

Playbook C · The Reference-Customer Engine

Expected output2 usable case studies

Why this exists

Existing small-retailer pilots are running informally. They need to be formalised before being used as sales references, to protect both sides. An informal relationship that sours leaves nothing usable in sales conversations, and potentially creates POPIA exposure.

The minimum viable formalisation

Written pilot agreement (3–6 months free, scope, liability limits), POPIA consent for data holding and deletion, named primary contact (person not shop), agreed success criteria (measurable), written case-study permission, pilot-to-paid conversion path with notice period.

Why this gates the other playbooks

Both the franchise and accounting-firm pitches get dramatically stronger when NuAi can say “here is the tenant who saved R240k on stock write-offs in their first quarter, quote available, site visit available.” Without formal pilots, those sentences are unusable.

Legal cost

R2–5k one-off for template drafting by a commercial attorney. Template reused across all future pilots. Amortised per pilot: negligible. Worth every rand to avoid the one pilot that sours and leaves a commercial mess.

Sequence

Week 1
Template drafted. Commercial attorney produces pilot agreement + POPIA consent + case-study release. Templates stored in the repo as docs/legal/PILOT-TEMPLATE_v1.md.
Week 2
Existing pilots formalised. Every informal pilot is converted to formal with written agreement, named contact, and agreed 90-day success metrics. Takes 30–60 minutes per pilot. Any pilot that will not sign the formalisation is not a pilot — it’s a courtesy access and gets written up that way.
Week 4
First case study published. One pilot with written permission becomes a two-page case study (challenge / implementation / numbers / quote). Shared on LinkedIn, embedded in the pitch document, sent as leave-behind to franchise meetings. This is the single highest-leverage one-page artefact in the GTM arsenal.
08 · Unit Economics

Know the per-tenant math before signing the first paid contract.

NuAi’s largest variable cost is the Anthropic API — every Smart Capture, every ingredient ingest, every ProteaAI query, every nightly loyalty job costs real money. This cost line has never been modelled. The table below is a directional model to be validated through the first paid contract. The principle is simple: COGS stays below 30 % of revenue per tenant at every usage tier, or pricing changes.

R150–R350 /mo

R450–R1,100 /mo
Franchise outlet · 10 invoices/day
R1,500–R3,500 /mo
Enterprise F&B · 50 invoices/day
R7,500–R17,000 /mo
Implied COGS discipline
< 30% of ARPU

Pricing tiers to match COGS curves

The work package WP-UNIT-ECONOMICS (logged in the repo at docs/PENDING-ACTIONS.md) formalises these numbers against actual Anthropic billing. Before first paid contract lands, a spreadsheet must exist that shows: per-operation input/output token cost for every Claude-calling edge function; per-tenant-month total at each usage scenario; pricing tiers (Starter / Growth / Enterprise) with ingest rate-caps that keep unit economics healthy.

Gatekeeper: WP-INGEST-RATE-LIMIT

A per-tenant daily cap on AI ingest calls (soft warn at 20/day, hard cap at 50/day) is prerequisite for any paid F&B contract. Without it, a single tenant bulk-uploading 200 invoices in an afternoon generates 200 API calls and destroys their contribution margin on day one. This work is logged, scoped at approximately 3 hours, and blocks revenue on the F&B vertical.

09 · What Doesn’t Exist Yet

The honest gap list. None block a pilot. All block enterprise sales.

A GTM document that only lists strengths is not a GTM document. This section exists because sophisticated buyers — franchise groups, accounting firms, investors — will ask about these items early. Addressing them proactively is faster than being caught by them in a due-diligence call.

Polish gaps (SME-acceptable, not enterprise-acceptable)

  • HQ Transfer atomicity: per-line loop, no transaction wrapper — partial failures possible.
  • ~100 RLS policies use inline user_profiles subqueries. Fragile under load. WP-RLS-HYGIENE logged.
  • POS VAT pipeline not writing to vat_transactions — R5k BS gap per tenant with amber banner.
  • Trial Balance Excel export missing (CAs want this for working papers). 2 hours.
  • Mobile Smart Capture for F&B ingredients deferred to Phase 2F.

Enterprise-readiness gaps

  • White-label theming partial; HQ not themable.
  • Real auth invitation flow (tenant-admin-onboards-staff) still manual.
  • No production email infrastructure (Resend integration scoped, not shipped).
  • No formal SLA, no pen-test, no SOC 2 — enterprise buyers ask.
  • No documented disaster-recovery procedures.
  • Yoco production keys waiting on CIPRO + nuai.co.za domain.

Commercial-readiness gaps

  • Unit-economics spreadsheet not modelled. Blocks pricing first contract.
  • Ingest rate-limit not enforced. Blocks first paid F&B tenant.
  • Existing pilots informal. Blocks case-study use.
  • No pitch deck, no demo video, no one-page leave-behind.
  • No LinkedIn / brand presence beyond the founder’s personal profile.

Organisational gaps

  • CAC / churn / retention: zero data. Every paid customer is also an econometric experiment.
  • Support tier model: nobody is defined as “the person who answers at 2am when cash-up fails.”
  • Onboarding playbooks: not written.
  • POPIA audit: not formally completed.
  • Terms of service / privacy policy / master agreement templates: drafts only.

The observation that matters. None of the polish or enterprise gaps block a 30-outlet franchise pilot. They become blockers only at the point someone signs a R500k+ annual contract and does proper due diligence. That moment is roughly 90–180 days away on the Playbook A timeline. These items are scheduled against that window, not against today.
